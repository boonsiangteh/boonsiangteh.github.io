Projects

Carleton University Supercritical Refrigerant Loop Project

This research project is a collaboration between Carleton University and Atomic Energy of Canada Ltd (AECL) as part of the latter's Gen IV nuclear reactor initiative where 4 test sections each consisting of different cross-sections were tested in the loop. As a Junior Mechanical Engineer in the project, I designed a mechanical traverse system to traverse a hot-wire anemometer in the test section to measure turbulence in a supercritical environment. The system was designed to withstand maximum pressures and temperatures of up to 7 MPa and 250 deg Celcius respectively.

Carleton University Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) Capstone Project

This is a final year capstone project in which an Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) was to be designed for the purpose of crop surveillance in Alberta and Saskatchewan. I was a member of the Design and Integration Group where I was responsible for developing the business and techncial requirements as well as reviewing all CAD submissions of my colleagues to ensure that all designs comply with the technical requirements. I was also responsible for coordinating all project meetings and design reviews for product improvement.

Takeoff and Landing System (TLS) Design Project

This group project was a coursework for the Aerospace Systems and Design course at Carleton University in which it was required that the Takeoff and Landing System (TLS) of the Boeing Insitu Integrator be redesigned for the purpose of Geophysical Surveillance.
